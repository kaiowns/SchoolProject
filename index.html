‚ò™Ô∏è Islam

Concept of God

Islam believes in one supreme God‚ÄîAllah. He is merciful, all-powerful, and just. The core of Islam is submission to Allah‚Äôs will.

Sacred Texts

The Quran is the holy book of Islam, revealed in Arabic to the Prophet Muhammad over 23 years. It is considered the final revelation from God.

Practices and Rituals

Muslims follow the Five Pillars: faith, prayer five times a day, almsgiving, fasting during Ramadan, and pilgrimage to Mecca. Daily life is infused with religious awareness.

Forgiveness

Forgiveness is emphasized. Muslims seek forgiveness through prayer and good deeds. Allah is described as the Most Forgiving and Most Merciful.

Roles in Society

Islam shapes family values, community, laws, and ethics in many societies. Mosques are centers of worship, education, and charity.

Learning Points

  • Faith is a way of life, not just belief.
  • Discipline and routine play key roles.
  • Community and unity are highly valued.

‚úùÔ∏è Christianity

Concept of God

Christians believe in one God in three forms: Father, Son (Jesus Christ), and Holy Spirit. God is love, and Jesus came to save humanity.

Sacred Texts

The Bible, divided into Old and New Testaments, records the journey of God's people and the teachings of Jesus Christ.

Practices and Rituals

Prayer, church services, singing hymns, and sacraments like baptism and communion are vital. Christmas and Easter celebrate key events in Jesus‚Äô life.

Forgiveness

Jesus taught unconditional forgiveness. Christians are taught to forgive others as God forgives them through grace.

Roles in Society

Christianity influences art, politics, and education. Churches support communities, help the poor, and guide moral decisions.

Learning Points

  • Love and compassion are central themes.
  • Jesus' teachings promote peace and humility.
  • Faith brings people hope and guidance.

ü™∑ Buddhism

Concept of God

Buddhism does not focus on a creator god. Instead, it teaches followers to reach enlightenment through mindfulness, ethics, and wisdom.

Sacred Texts

Texts like the Tripitaka and various sutras contain the teachings of Siddhartha Gautama, the Buddha, and guide practitioners in the Eightfold Path.

Practices and Rituals

Meditation, chanting, and observing precepts help individuals reduce suffering and reach nirvana. Temples and retreats are common for practice.

Forgiveness

Forgiveness is seen as a way to free the mind from hatred. Letting go is crucial for inner peace and karma cleansing.

Roles in Society

Buddhism influences architecture, art, and social harmony. Monks often act as spiritual guides and community helpers.

Learning Points

  • Peace comes from within.
  • Letting go of attachment leads to happiness.
  • Life is about balance, not extremes.

‚ú°Ô∏è Judaism

Concept of God

Jews believe in one eternal God‚ÄîYahweh‚Äîwho is deeply involved in human history. God is righteous, just, and has a covenant with the Jewish people.

Sacred Texts

The Torah is the core text, followed by writings like the Talmud. These texts guide Jewish life, law, and ethics.

Practices and Rituals

Observing the Sabbath, kosher laws, bar/bat mitzvahs, and holidays like Hanukkah and Passover are key rituals in Jewish tradition.

Forgiveness

Forgiveness is taken seriously. Jews must ask and grant forgiveness before Yom Kippur. Atonement requires effort and sincerity.

Roles in Society

Judaism has influenced law, philosophy, and ethics. Jewish communities emphasize education, charity, and remembrance.

Learning Points

  • History and identity are central.
  • Law and tradition shape everyday life.
  • Remembrance and resilience define the Jewish spirit.
