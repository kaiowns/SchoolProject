Diversity in Religion

A comparative presentation project on major world religions

Introduction to Diversity

Religious diversity enriches societies by fostering understanding, tolerance, and cultural exchange. While different in beliefs, many religions aim to answer life's big questions, guiding billions in values, purpose, and morality.

1. Concept of God

Islam

Monotheistic belief in Allah, the singular, all-powerful creator. God is merciful and just.

Christianity

Belief in one God in three persons: the Father, the Son (Jesus), and the Holy Spirit.

Buddhism

No central god; rather, focus on spiritual enlightenment through understanding and meditation.

Judaism

Strict monotheism; belief in Yahweh as the one and only God of Israel.

2. Books and Sacred Texts

Islam

The Quran, believed to be the literal word of God revealed to Prophet Muhammad.

Christianity

The Bible, divided into the Old Testament and the New Testament, teachings of Jesus and prophets.

Buddhism

Tripitaka, the teachings of the Buddha, and various sutras depending on the tradition.

Judaism

The Torah (first five books of the Bible), along with the Talmud and other rabbinical writings.

3. Practices and Rituals

Islam

Five Pillars: Shahada, Salat, Zakat, Sawm, and Hajj. Daily prayers and fasting during Ramadan.

Christianity

Prayer, church attendance, sacraments (e.g., baptism, communion), and religious holidays.

Buddhism

Meditation, chanting, mindfulness, monastic life, and observance of ethical precepts.

Judaism

Keeping kosher, Sabbath observance, prayer, festivals like Passover and Yom Kippur.

4. Forgiveness in Religions

Islam

Allah is all-forgiving. Repentance and seeking forgiveness are encouraged through prayer and good deeds.

Christianity

Forgiveness is central. Jesus taught to forgive others and that God forgives all who repent.

Buddhism

Forgiveness is vital for inner peace. No concept of divine forgiveness, but letting go of resentment is emphasized.

Judaism

Forgiveness from God requires repentance. Asking and granting forgiveness is essential, especially on Yom Kippur.

5. Conclusions: Roles in Society and Culture

Religions shape laws, art, architecture, holidays, education, and values. They foster community identity and offer moral guidance. Each religion has had a profound influence on history and culture in their regions and globally.

6. Learning Points

  • Appreciation of diverse beliefs fosters tolerance and global understanding.
  • Core values like compassion, forgiveness, and community are shared across religions.
  • Understanding religious rituals deepens cultural awareness.
  • Despite differences, many religions aim to guide moral and ethical living.
